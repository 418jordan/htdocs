Fun Seal Facts

  1. Seals are believed to have evolved from land based, bear or otter-like ancestors.

  2. There are around 33 species of seals.

  3. Seals are found in most waters of the world, mainly in the Arctic and Antarctic but also in some areas of the tropics.

  4. A seal's whiskers help it to detect prey in dark murky waters.

  5. Seals live on average for 25 - 30 years, females usually live longer than males.

  6. Seals mainly live in the water, they only come ashore to mate, give birth, moult or escape from predators such as orca whales and sharks.
